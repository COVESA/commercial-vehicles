# Fleet Vehicle Sales Estimates by Region and Vehicle Class

**Reference period:** 2023–2024 annual estimates  
**Last updated:** April 2026  
**Note:** Figures are estimates synthesized from industry reports, market research, and trade publications. "Fleet" includes corporate/commercial fleets, government, rental, public transit, logistics, and construction. Figures represent *new vehicle sales to fleet customers* — not total vehicle stock. Not all commercial vehicle data is publicly disaggregated by fleet vs. retail; methodology notes are at the bottom.

---

## Vehicle Class Definitions

| Class | Definition | Examples |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Buses** | Full-size transit, school, coach, and intercity buses (typically >25 seats) | School bus, city transit bus, motorcoach |
| **Heavy-Duty Trucks** | GVW >15 tonnes / Class 7–8 (US) | Semi-trucks, tractor-trailers, heavy haulers |
| **Medium-Duty Trucks** | GVW 6–15 tonnes / Class 4–6 (US) | Box trucks, refuse trucks, flatbeds, tankers |
| **Pickup Trucks (Fleet)** | Light-duty pickups sold to commercial/fleet buyers | Ford F-Series, RAM 1500, Toyota Hilux, Isuzu D-Max |
| **Delivery Vans / Utility Vehicles** | Cargo vans and minivans used for last-mile delivery and service | Ford Transit, Mercedes Sprinter, Peugeot Boxer, Renault Trafic |
| **Fleet Passenger Cars / SUVs** | Cars and SUVs sold through corporate, government, and rental channels | Sedans, CUVs, SUVs sold to fleet accounts |

**Key observations:**
- Buses, HD trucks, MD trucks, and delivery vans are essentially 100% fleet/commercial — the individual buyer market is negligible
- Pickup trucks are the most variable: North America's low fleet rate (~20–25%) reflects consumer popularity; in Africa, the Middle East, and Europe they are purchased primarily as work vehicles (~60–75% fleet)
- Passenger cars are the only class where private retail dominates in every region; Europe's ~37% fleet rate is notably high vs. Asia's ~12%

## Regional Narratives

### North America
- **Total new vehicle market:** ~19–20 million units/year (US ~16.3M, Canada ~1.8M, Mexico ~1.5M in 2024)
- **Fleet penetration (passenger cars/SUVs):** ~12–13% of US retail auto sales are fleet; US fleet total ~2.1M (2024): rental ~49%, commercial ~42%, government ~9%
- **Heavy-duty trucks (Class 7–8):** US net orders ~280–320K/year (Class 8 alone); US+Canada total ~300–350K; owner-operators are counted as fleet operators but are one-truck businesses
- **Medium-duty trucks (Class 4–6):** US ~200–250K/year; Class 5–6 segment includes delivery box trucks, service trucks, and dump trucks
- **Pickup trucks to fleet:** Enormous segment in the US; F-150 alone sells ~750K/year with significant fleet share; RAM 2500/3500, Chevy Silverado commercial buyers; est. 600–800K pickups/year to commercial fleets
- **Delivery vans:** Ford Transit (138K in 2024, ~60% full-size van share), plus RAM ProMaster, Mercedes Sprinter, Chevy Express; US total ~230–250K full-size + smaller commercial vans; total est. 400–600K
- **Buses:** US ~70–80K (school ~33K, transit ~8K, motorcoach ~5K, charter/shuttle); Canada ~10–15K
- **Key countries:** United States, Canada, Mexico

### Europe
- **Total new vehicle market:** ~12–13 million units/year (EU27 ~10.6M, UK ~1.6M, Norway/Switzerland ~0.3M)
- **Fleet penetration (passenger cars/SUVs):** ~36–38% of all new car registrations; France highest at ~53%; corporate fleets + short-term rental collectively ~58% in some markets; est. 4.2–4.8M passenger fleet cars/year
- **Heavy-duty trucks (>16 tonnes):** EU+UK ~280–350K/year (ACEA data); Germany, Poland, France lead; sharp decline in 2024 due to freight softness
- **Medium-duty trucks (6–16 tonnes):** EU ~150–200K; includes rigid trucks, municipal vehicles, construction
- **Pickup trucks to fleet:** Much smaller segment than North America; ~50–100K/year, growing in UK, Nordics, Eastern Europe (Toyota Hilux, Ford Ranger)
- **Delivery vans (≤3.5 tonnes):** EU alone: ~1.5M in 2023 (+14.6% YoY), ~400K in Q1 2024 (+12.6%); Italy, Germany, Spain, France lead; nearly all are fleet purchases; includes Renault Trafic, Peugeot Boxer, Ford Transit, VW Transporter, Mercedes Sprinter
- **Buses:** EU+UK ~40–60K/year; Germany, France, UK, Spain largest; large electric bus deployment underway
- **Key countries:** Germany, France, UK, Poland, Spain, Italy

### Asia
- **Total new vehicle market:** ~51–55 million units/year; China alone ~25–30M; India ~4.5M; Japan ~4.8M; South Korea ~1.5M; SE Asia ~3.5M
- **Fleet penetration (passenger cars/SUVs):** ~10–15% average across region; Japan/South Korea ~20%; China ~10–12%
- **Heavy-duty trucks:** China alone sold ~911K heavy trucks in 2023; India ~300–350K; SE Asia/Japan/Korea ~200K; Asia-Pacific accounts for ~47–51% of global heavy-duty truck production
- **Medium-duty trucks:** China and India large markets; Japan's medium-duty fleet globally recognized (Hino, Fuso); est. 500–800K region-wide
- **Pickup trucks to fleet:** Growing rapidly in SE Asia and Australia; Toyota Hilux, Isuzu D-Max dominant; ~200–400K fleet pickups; Australia/NZ alone ~100K
- **Delivery vans:** China electric LCV sales exceeded 450K in 2024 alone; total Asia delivery van fleet sales ~1.5–2M including conventional ICE; China leads, India growing
- **Buses:** China delivers ~400K new buses/year (dominant globally); India ~100–150K; SE Asia, Japan, Korea ~50–100K; Asia-Pacific represents >60% of global bus volume
- **Key countries:** China, India, Japan, South Korea, Australia, Indonesia, Thailand

### Latin America
- **Total new vehicle market:** ~4.8–5.2M units/year; Brazil ~2.4M, Mexico ~1.5M lead
- **Fleet penetration (passenger cars/SUVs):** ~12–15%; Brazil leads with fleet-heavy corporate market
- **Commercial vehicles Brazil:** 587K commercial vehicles sold in 2023 (largest in region); light commercial = ~66% share
- **Heavy-duty trucks:** Brazil, Mexico, Argentina main markets; Brazilian truck market historically strong; regional total ~100–150K
- **Medium-duty trucks:** ~100–150K; Brazil and Mexico dominate
- **Pickup trucks to fleet:** Significant in Brazil (Chevrolet S10, Fiat Strada), Mexico, and Andean markets; ~150–200K to fleet
- **Delivery vans:** ~200–300K; growing with e-commerce expansion; Volkswagen Delivery, Ford Transit widely used
- **Buses:** Brazil is one of world's largest bus markets; fleet total ~80–120K region-wide; urban, school, and intercity
- **Key countries:** Brazil, Mexico, Argentina, Colombia, Chile

### Middle East
- **Total commercial vehicle market:** ~$29–38B market value; LCVs dominant at 55–73% of units sold
- **Fleet penetration:** High in Gulf (GCC); construction, logistics, oil & gas drive demand; government fleet spending substantial
- **Heavy-duty trucks:** ~60–80K/year medium-heavy; Saudi Arabia, UAE, Turkey lead; construction boom driving demand
- **Medium-duty trucks:** ~40–70K; building materials, utilities, government
- **Pickup trucks to fleet:** Very popular in Gulf states and construction; Toyota Land Cruiser/Hilux, Mitsubishi L200 dominant; ~80–100K fleet pickups
- **Delivery vans:** ~100–150K/year; logistics and last-mile growing; e-commerce expansion in UAE, KSA
- **Buses:** ~20–30K/year; urban transit expanding in Riyadh, Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Doha
- **Fleet passenger cars:** Government and corporate fleets, rental; ~200–300K/year
- **Key countries:** Saudi Arabia, UAE, Qatar, Kuwait, Turkey (partial Middle East alignment)

### Africa
- **Market characteristics:** Fragmented; limited local production; significant second-hand imports not captured here (new only)
- **Heavy-duty trucks:** South Africa, Kenya, Nigeria, Egypt lead for new truck sales; ~50–80K/year; mining and long-haul dominate
- **Medium-duty trucks:** ~30–50K; construction, municipal, agriculture
- **Pickup trucks to fleet:** Toyota Hilux widely dominant across the continent; construction, agriculture, government; ~50–80K fleet
- **Delivery vans:** ~50–80K; South Africa largest formal delivery van market
- **Buses:** ~30–50K new; South Africa, Nigeria, Kenya formal transit; large informal matatu/minibus sector excluded
- **Fleet passenger cars:** ~100–150K; corporate, government, rental (tourism)
- **Key countries:** South Africa, Nigeria, Kenya, Egypt, Morocco, Ethiopia

## Methodology Notes

1. **"Fleet" definition:** Vehicles sold to corporate accounts, government, rental companies, transit authorities, logistics firms, construction, and utilities. Excludes private/retail purchases.
2. **Trucks and buses** are treated as ~100% fleet by nature; even owner-operators are fleet customers for telematics purposes.
3. **Pickup trucks** are split: only units sold to identified fleet/commercial buyers are counted, not retail private buyers. In North America the fleet share of pickup sales is estimated at ~20–25%; in other regions pickups are more predominantly commercial.
4. **Delivery vans** in Europe are nearly 100% fleet/commercial. In North America the share is high (~85–90%) but some are purchased privately.
5. **Passenger car fleet share** uses reported figures where available (Europe ~36%, US ~12–13%) and regional proxies elsewhere.
6. **Africa and Middle East figures** involve significant uncertainty due to limited formal market data and large informal/second-hand markets.
7. **Asia range is wide** due to variability across China (large retail base), Japan/Korea (higher fleet %), and developing SE Asian markets.
8. **Not included:** Second-hand vehicles, informal sector (e.g. African matatus), government vehicle stock not sold in reporting year.
